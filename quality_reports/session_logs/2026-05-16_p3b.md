## Entry 8 — Mechanism deepening: bargaining vs composition

Per user request (after P3b-1 results presentation), ran `explorations/2026-05-16_p3b-mechanism/check.R` to decompose the −11.1% pass-through into causal channels:

**(A) Composition (area_rent change):** treated farms reduce rented area by 851 m² (T1), 548 (T2), 39 (T3). All sign-consistent negative but **non-significant** (p > 0.2 throughout). Modest mechanical contribution.

**(B) Bargaining (unit_rent_price = rent_cost / area_rent among renters):** **T1 −67 KRW/m², p = 0.0498 (exactly 5% significant)**. T2 +3 (null), T3 −57 (p=0.36). **Bargaining hypothesis confirmed at narrow bandwidth — tenant unit-price burden declines under policy.**

**(C) Market dynamics:** absorbed by year FE in DiD-RD spec.

**Area expansion decomposition (T3 event-study):**
- 2022 area_total +408 m² = area_own +237 m² (p=0.012**, **58% of expansion**) + area_rent +171 (p=0.144, 42%)
- Treated farms expand **primarily via own-cultivation** (자작), not via rental
- Static panel DiD-RD shows area_total β ≈ 0 (hides dynamic pattern via pre/post averaging)
- **Event-study is the correct framing**, not static panel coefficient

**Parallel trends gate:** all 2018 pre-period coefficients across area_total / area_own / area_rent / rent_cost have |t| < 1 → RD identification strong, Roth-Rambachan sensitivity unlikely to bind.
