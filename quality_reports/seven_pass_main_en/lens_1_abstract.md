## Score: 6/10

The abstract is dense, technically precise, and accurate to the body — but it buries the research question behind a textbook lecture, omits the headline policy-implication sentence required for a journal abstract, and over-spends words on identification minutiae (the 14.6% non-compliance parenthetical, the 2018-baseline freeze, the "$\bar D = 0.312$" mean) that belong in §4, not the abstract. The first sentence is a paragraph-textbook definition of the AHM null rather than the research question. The contribution sentence is missing entirely. The headline number system is split awkwardly — the F1 area gradient is quantified for the pure-tenant only (no monotone series), and the (S,s) op_cost coefficient is reported without its sister rent-cost incidence-reversal (−12.0%, the contrast against Kirwan/Ciaian that the body actually leans on for the policy contribution). This is rewrite-required for AJAE desk-review survival.

## CRITICAL findings

- **C1 — First sentence is not the research question.** The opening sentence is a textbook definition: *"The Agricultural Household Model's separability null states that smallholder production and consumption decisions are recursive under complete markets, so a lump-sum transfer should not shift production."* AJAE/JAE/Food Policy editors making 30-second desk-review decisions need the **question** in sentence 1, not a definitional preamble. The §1 intro on line 48 opens correctly with *"Does the Agricultural Household Model's separability null hold for Korean smallholders at the 0.5~ha cutoff of a per-farm flat-rate direct payment, and through which mechanism does it fail if rejected?"* — that question (or a declarative version of it) belongs at the top of the abstract. **Fix:** Open with *"We test whether the Agricultural Household Model's separability null holds at the 0.5~ha eligibility cutoff of Korea's per-farm flat-rate direct-payment scheme."* Move the textbook definition to sentence 2 or cut entirely.

- **C2 — No one-sentence contribution statement.** The rubric requires a contribution sentence positioning the paper against Kirwan (2009) and Ciaian/Baldoni-Ciaian (2023). The abstract names both papers — *"\citep{Kirwan2009_rentcap, BaldoniCiaian2023_eucap}"* on line 34 — but only to characterize the institutional contrast (per-farm flat vs. area-proportional). It never says *what is new*. The §1 intro on line 58 cleanly states the three contributions (methodological / theoretical / empirical); none of this reaches the abstract. A reader of the abstract alone learns we *tested* separability but not *what AJAE should publish that no prior paper delivers*. **Fix:** Add a sentence like *"This is the first AHM separability test exploiting a per-farm flat-rate direct-payment cutoff, and the first DiD-RD evidence on Korea's PIDPS; we extend the wealth-biased liquidity framework of Carter-Olinto (2003) from a land-titling to a direct-payment setting."*

- **C3 — No policy-implications preview.** The rubric item 5 (policy implications) is absent. The Discussion §7 (line 446) carries a load-bearing policy point — *"The pure-tenant own-cultivated-area response of $+1{,}122$~m\textsuperscript{2} at T2 is approximately 22\% of the 0.5~ha eligibility threshold"* — and the rent-cost pass-through reverses Kirwan and Baldoni-Ciaian (line 448). Neither makes it to the abstract. For a journal where policy salience drives acceptance (especially Food Policy as the cascade fallback), this is unforced error. **Fix:** Close with one sentence: *"Per-farm flat-rate direct payments can produce land-tenure shifts among credit-constrained smallholders without the landlord rent-capitalization observed under area-proportional U.S.\ and EU CAP designs."*

## MAJOR findings

- **M1 — Headline quantification is incomplete and split awkwardly.** The abstract reports the pure-tenant point estimate *"$+1{,}122$~m\textsuperscript{2} at the T2 bandwidth ($p=.041$)"* and says it is *"monotone-decreasing across four non-pure-owner bins"* — but does not give the gradient numerically. The body §5 line 350 reports the full monotone series ($+1{,}122 \to +403 \to +222 \to -74 \to 0$). The monotone gradient is the load-bearing feature of the wealth-bias rejection (per §5 ¶ "F1 result"), so omitting the bin-by-bin numbers undersells the paper's central diagnostic. The reduced-form rent-cost pass-through of −12.0% (§5 line 363; §7 line 448) — which is the entire basis for the Kirwan/Ciaian incidence-reversal contrast — is **absent from the abstract entirely**. **Fix:** Report the four-bin gradient compactly (*"$+1{,}122 \to +403 \to +222 \to -74$~m\textsuperscript{2}"*) and add the −12.0% rent-cost line.

- **M2 — Over-spends words on identification trivia at the expense of headline.** The clause *"the three FHES-observable Small-Farmer Flat Payment criteria, 14.6\% non-compliance among area-eligibles dropped"* (38 words including the surrounding "the statutorily-eligible subset" parenthetical) is detail that belongs in §4 (line 54). Same for *"exploiting the 2018-baseline freeze"* — a desk-reviewer does not need the freeze mechanism in the abstract. These cost roughly 50 words that should be carrying the contribution sentence (C2) and policy preview (C3). **Fix:** Compress to *"We estimate the intent-to-treat effect of statutory SFFP eligibility (three FHES-observable criteria, fixed at the 2018 baseline) in a difference-in-differences regression-discontinuity design on a Farm Household Economic Survey panel ($N=13{,}689$ farm-years; $3{,}420$ farms; 2018--2022)."*

- **M3 — Theoretical scaffolding in abstract is heavier than the empirical content it justifies.** The abstract spends three sentences setting up two AHM extensions with citations to Carter-Olinto and Eswaran-Kotwal (line 34, "Two AHM extensions yield falsifiable predictions...") before delivering one sentence of results. The F2 (supervision) channel is named as "informative-not-rejecting" but the abstract never reports an F2 number or even says F2 came up null — the reader is left wondering if F2 fired. The body §5 ¶ "F2 result" (line 361) is clear that F2 is null. **Fix:** Either drop F2 from the abstract entirely (it does not bear the rejection) or state in one clause that it was null and uninformative under sign indeterminacy.

- **M4 — The headline conclusion sentence is buried as the last line and is weaker than it needs to be.** *"We reject the AHM separability null for Korean small farms via the wealth-biased liquidity channel."* — this is the actual headline of the paper and it lands in position 8 of 8 sentences. Compare to §5 line 367 *"\textit{We reject AHM separability for Korean small farms at the 0.5~ha cutoff.}"* The abstract version omits *"at the 0.5~ha cutoff"* — the identification anchor — and arrives after the reader has already absorbed seven sentences of setup. **Fix:** Move closer to the top, after the question sentence, restored to the §5 wording with the cutoff anchor.

## Verbatim abstract

> The Agricultural Household Model's separability null states that smallholder production and consumption decisions are recursive under complete markets, so a lump-sum transfer should not shift production. We test this at Korea's 2020 Public-Interest Direct Payment Scheme, which transfers 1.2 million KRW per year to smallholders at a 0.5~ha cultivated-area threshold under a per-farm flat-rate design---distinct from the area-proportional U.S.\ and EU CAP schedules \citep{Kirwan2009_rentcap, BaldoniCiaian2023_eucap}. We estimate the intent-to-treat effect on the statutorily-eligible subset (the three FHES-observable Small-Farmer Flat Payment criteria, 14.6\% non-compliance among area-eligibles dropped), exploiting the 2018-baseline freeze in a difference-in-differences regression-discontinuity design on a Farm Household Economic Survey panel ($N = 13{,}689$ farm-years, $3{,}420$ farms, 2018--2022). Two AHM extensions yield falsifiable predictions: wealth-biased liquidity \citep{CarterOlinto2003_liquidity} predicts a monotone-in-baseline-tenancy gradient on own-cultivated area (F1, load-bearing); implicit-labor supervision \citep{EswaranKotwal1986_supervision} predicts a hired-labor margin response (F2, informative-not-rejecting). The pure-tenant own-area response is $+1{,}122$~m\textsuperscript{2} at the T2 bandwidth ($p=.041$), monotone-decreasing across four non-pure-owner bins, with a negative operating-cost-net-of-rent response of $-3.98$~M~KRW at T1 ($p=.057$) consistent with $(S,s)$ disinvestment. The headline coefficients are stable to within 2.2\% under the area-only treatment definition. We reject the AHM separability null for Korean small farms via the wealth-biased liquidity channel.

## Recommended rewrite

> We test the Agricultural Household Model's separability null at the 0.5~ha eligibility cutoff of Korea's 2020 Public-Interest Direct Payment Scheme — a per-farm flat-rate transfer of 1.2 million KRW/year, institutionally distinct from the area-proportional U.S.\ and EU CAP schedules. Exploiting a 2018-baseline-frozen running variable, we estimate the intent-to-treat effect of statutory eligibility in a difference-in-differences regression-discontinuity design on a Farm Household Economic Survey panel ($N = 13{,}689$ farm-years; $3{,}420$ farms; 2018--2022). The pure-tenant own-cultivated-area response is $+1{,}122$~m\textsuperscript{2} at $\pm 1{,}000$~m\textsuperscript{2} ($p=.041$) and monotone-decreasing across baseline-tenancy bins ($1{,}122 \to 403 \to 222 \to -74 \to 0$~m\textsuperscript{2}) — a signature uniquely consistent with the Carter--Olinto wealth-biased liquidity channel and incompatible with a universal income-effect alternative. Operating cost net of rent contracts by $-3.98$~M~KRW at the narrow bandwidth ($p=.057$), consistent with the $(S,s)$ inaction band at the calibrated transfer-to-adjustment-cost ratio. The reduced-form rent-cost pass-through is $-12.0\%$, reversing the $+25\%$ and $+46$--$55\%$ landlord rent-capitalization documented by Kirwan (2009) for U.S.\ direct payments and by Baldoni-Ciaian (2023) for EU CAP Pillar I. This is the first AHM separability test at a per-farm flat-rate direct-payment cutoff, the first DiD-RD evidence on Korean PIDPS, and a developed-country complement to the Benjamin-LaFave-Thomas demographic-instrument tradition. We reject AHM separability via the wealth-biased liquidity channel; per-farm flat-rate transfers can shift the land-tenure margin without the landlord rent-capitalization observed under area-proportional designs.
