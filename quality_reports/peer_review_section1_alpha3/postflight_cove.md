## Discrepancies requiring action

### **C5 (CRITICAL) — Country attribution error (Paraguay vs Honduras)**

- **Draft says:** "Carter and Olinto (2003), AJAE 85(1):173–186, formalize wealth-biased liquidity using **rural-Honduras land-titling** intervention data."
- **Source says:** The paper uses **Paraguay** panel data, not Honduras. The Honduras land-titling study is Boucher, Barham, and Carter (2005) in *World Development* — a separate paper.
- **Fix required:** Change "rural-Honduras" → "rural-Paraguay" in §1 (and anywhere else this attribution appears). The wealth-biased liquidity mechanism + cross-partial signature claim itself IS correct; only the country is wrong.
- **Severity:** This is a citation-fidelity error. Per quality_gates.md, factual citation errors are Major (−10) at minimum. Recommend surgical fix.

### **C9 (MINOR) — Year convention (2022 vs 2023)**

- **Draft says:** "Zimmert, Franz and Zorn, Alexander (2022)..."
- **Source convention:** The article appeared online 29 August 2022 (Advance Article) but is bound in Volume 3 Issue 3, which is the **2023** volume. Both DOAJ and Oxford Academic's canonical citation use **2023**.
- **Fix:** Change year to 2023 in BibTeX entry AND in-text citation, unless the author has a specific reason to use online-first year. Standard economics-journal citation practice favors volume year.
- **Severity:** Minor (–3 to –5). Bibliography-correctness gate.

### **C2 (MINOR) — Range characterization**

- **Draft says:** "Baldoni and Ciaian (2023), Land Use Policy, find 46–55% pass-through."
- **Source says:** Baldoni & Ciaian's range is broader: short-run 9.1–46.2%, long-run 11–55%. The "46–55%" cherry-picks the upper bounds.
- **Fix:** Either (a) reword to "up to 46% short-run / 55% long-run" or "long-run upper bound 55%"; or (b) explicitly state these are upper-bound estimates. The current phrasing implies a tight 46–55% range which may mislead a referee who checks the paper.
- **Severity:** Minor (–3). Defensible if rephrased; recommend revise.
