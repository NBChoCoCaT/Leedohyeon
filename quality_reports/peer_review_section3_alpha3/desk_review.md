## One-paragraph contribution statement (my understanding)

The paper exploits Korea's 0.5 ha eligibility cutoff in the per-farm flat-rate Small-Farmer Flat Payment (SFFP) to test the Singh–Squire–Strauss (1986) AHM separability null in a DiD-RD design. Two AHM extensions are formalized as closed-form predictions with stated sufficient conditions: (i) Carter-Olinto (2003) wealth-biased liquidity, which predicts an own-area expansion $\partial A_{own}/\partial T_{SFFP}>0$ with a monotone-in-baseline-tenancy gradient (the empirical signature uniquely diagnostic of wealth-bias and not of a universal income effect), plus a capital-adjustment sub-prediction $\partial \text{op\_cost\_ex\_rent}/\partial T \le 0$ when $T_{SFFP}/\tau \approx 0.048 \ll 1$; (ii) Eswaran-Kotwal (1986) supervision, auxiliary, predicting $\partial\,\text{off\_farm\_income}/\partial T \ne 0$ with theoretically indeterminate sign. Two falsification triggers (F1 for the gradient, F2 for the supervision margin) are pre-registered with explicit reframes — including a "precise developed-country null estimate complementing LaFave-Thomas (2016) ECMA" reframe if F1+F2 both fire. The contribution is the joint cutoff-design test of AHM separability in a developed-country smallholder setting that mostly leaves the demographic-instrument literature (Benjamin 1992, LaFave-Thomas 2016) intact while substituting a sharp policy threshold for the demographic identification.
